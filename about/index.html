About the Ministry of Culture

Introduction

The Ministry of Culture, Government of India, works to preserve, protect, and promote the nation’s heritage and cultural expressions. It supports institutions and initiatives that keep traditional knowledge, arts, and history accessible to citizens.

Through policies, partnerships, and public programs, the ministry strengthens cultural infrastructure and encourages participation in arts and heritage across regions.

Key Responsibilities

  • Conservation and restoration of monuments and heritage sites.
  • Support for museums, archives, libraries, and cultural institutions.
  • Promotion of performing arts, folk traditions, crafts, and literature.
  • Implementation of cultural schemes, grants, and fellowships.
  • Documentation and safeguarding of intangible cultural heritage.

Vision & Mission

Vision

A culturally confident India where heritage is preserved and the arts are valued, accessible, and participatory.

Mission

To strengthen cultural institutions, support artists and communities, and create programs that protect heritage and encourage learning, research, and cultural exchange.

Organizational Overview

The ministry coordinates with cultural bodies, institutions, and partner organizations to deliver schemes and services. Its work spans heritage conservation, cultural education, and support for creative communities, ensuring programs are implemented consistently and responsibly.
